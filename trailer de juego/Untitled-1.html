Monster Hunter World

is a video game belonging to the action role-playing genre, developed and published by the Capcom company, being the sixth main title in the Monster Hunter video game franchise. The game was announced at Sony's E3 2017 conference. The game features much larger stages, seamless transitions between areas on the map, and online cooperative mode for up to four players. It will also allow players around the world to play together, being the first in the series with these features. In March 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, Capcom gave the game to PlayStation users in Japan who had PlayStation Plus. . as part of the campaign for players to stay home during quarantine.

Monster Hunter: World is considered a mainline entry in the Monster Hunter series, according to the game's producer Ryozo Tsujimoto and co-director Yuya Tokuda. Tokuda and co-director Kaname Fujioka were also directors of Monster Hunter 4 and Monster Hunter 4 Ultimate. Development of World began about three years before the reveal at E3 2017, after a year of brainstorming about what the game would be. next main game in the series. With the series over a decade old, Capcom reevaluated where they wanted to pick up the series, and realized that with the hardware capabilities of the new consoles, they could realize a different vision compared to those seen on personal computers.

Gameplay

Monster Hunter: World is an action role-playing video game, set in an open world environment, and played from a third-person perspective. As in previous games in the series, the player takes the role of a "Hunter" who is tasked by the "Commission of Inquiry", to pursue and kill (or capture) large monsters, which wander outside of their Astera central base for them to study. The player character does not have any intrinsic attributes, but is instead determined by the equipment the character is equipped with.

This includes a weapon that comes from one of the fourteen archetypes (such as longsword, bow, or hammer), which then defines the types of combat moves and skills the player can benefit from. The player can also equip himself with armor (ornaments can be added to this equipment, which are objects that provide different abilities) that have benefits or base abilities and that can be enhanced with decorations and ciguas, which are equipment elements with the same function. of the decorations except that it is only possible to equip one cigua at a time. While some basic equipment can be purchased with in-game currency, most equipment is built from materials obtained by killing or catching monsters, in rewards for completing quests, or items collected in the field. This creates a central loop of gameplay that the player has to fight monsters they can defeat, to obtain the correct loot to create upgraded weapons and armor to allow them to take on more powerful monsters, and the ability to have a team. Launch

At Tokyo Game Show 2017, Capcom confirmed the console's release dates for January 26, 2018. In addition to the regular digital and physical versions, the game will ship with a collector's edition that includes an art book, a CD with the game's soundtrack, and a statue of one of the game's monsters. A limited number of PlayStation 4 Pro consoles embellished with Monster Hunter: World artwork were released in Japan only on December 7, 2017.
